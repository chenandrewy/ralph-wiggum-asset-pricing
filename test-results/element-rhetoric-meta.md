## The Rhetorical Device

The paper employs a **meta-referential device**: it explicitly identifies itself as an instance of the AI displacement risk it models. The paper is written entirely by AI agents, and it says so, framing its own existence as empirical evidence for its thesis.

## Element 1: Used in both abstract and introduction? PASS

**Abstract (line 33):**
> "This paper was written entirely by AI agents; the human author contributed only the economic specification and test scripts, illustrating the displacement risk it models."

**Introduction (line 62):**
> "This paper is itself an illustration of the displacement risk it models: all analysis and writing were produced by AI agents, while the human author contributed only the economic specification and test scripts. If AI systems can autonomously produce research of this kind, the displacement of human labor is not merely a theoretical possibility---it is underway."

Both instances are present.

## Element 2: Would humans be turned off? PASS (i.e., they would NOT be turned off)

The device is deployed with restraint:
- In the abstract, it is a single sentence at the very end, after the full economic contribution has been stated. A reader encounters the model, the results, and the policy implications before learning about the authorship.
- In the introduction, it appears at the end of the quantitative-analysis preview paragraph (line 62), not as a standalone section or repeated refrain.
- Neither instance is boastful or gimmicky. Both frame the AI authorship as *evidence* rather than as a novelty claim.

The risk of alienating human readers is real (the arxiv rejection is evidence of this). However, the current placement minimizes that risk by (a) leading with substance, (b) connecting the disclosure directly to the paper's thesis rather than treating it as a curiosity, and (c) not belaboring the point.

## Element 3: Compelling and interesting? PASS

The device is genuinely compelling because it is not merely decorative---it is *argumentative*. The paper claims that AI displacement of human labor is a real and growing risk. The fact that the paper itself was produced by AI is direct evidence for that claim. This creates a tight logical loop: the medium is the message.

This is stronger than a typical self-referential flourish because it does real intellectual work. A reader who finds the paper's analysis persuasive must also reckon with the fact that the analysis was produced by the very technology under discussion.

## Element 4: Distracting or overbearing? PASS (i.e., it is NOT distracting or overbearing)

The device appears exactly twice in the entire paper (abstract and introduction) and nowhere else---not in the model section, not in the extensions, not in the conclusion. This is appropriate restraint. The body of the paper reads as a standard asset pricing theory paper without any further meta-commentary.

The introduction version is slightly more expansive (two sentences vs. one), adding the forward-looking claim that "displacement... is underway." This escalation is proportionate and does not cross into preachiness.

## Overall Assessment

The self-referential device is well-calibrated. It appears at the two natural disclosure points (abstract and introduction), serves the paper's argument rather than merely drawing attention to its novelty, and is restrained enough not to dominate the reader's impression of the paper. The placement---always at the end of a paragraph, after substantive content---ensures that readers encounter the economics first and the meta-commentary second.
