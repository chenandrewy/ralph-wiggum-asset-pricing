## Element 1: Used in both abstract and introduction?
**PASS.** The device appears in two places:

## Element 2: Would humans be turned off?
**PASS.** The placement is well chosen---both mentions appear at the *end* of their respective sections rather than leading with the disclosure. This matters: the reader encounters the economic argument first and the meta-observation second, so the device reads as evidence rather than self-promotion. The abstract mention is a single sentence. The introduction mention is two sentences that pivot directly into the paper's thesis. Neither interrupts the flow of economic reasoning. However, the phrasing "written entirely by AI agents" in the abstract is quite direct; a more cautious formulation (e.g., "drafted" or "produced") might reduce the risk of triggering the same skepticism that led to the arxiv rejection. This is a minor concern, not a failure.

## Element 3: Compelling and interesting?
**PASS.** The device is genuinely clever: a paper about AI displacement risk that is itself produced by AI is its own case study. The introduction version is especially effective because it connects the meta-observation back to the model's predictions ("the displacement of human labor is not merely a theoretical possibility---it is underway"). This transforms what could be a gimmick into a substantive point that reinforces the paper's argument. The accurate description of the division of labor (human wrote the spec and tests, AI did everything else) adds credibility.

## Element 4: Distracting or overbearing?
**PASS.** The device appears exactly twice---once in the abstract, once in the introduction---and nowhere else in the paper body. It does not recur in the model section, extensions, or conclusion. Each mention is brief (one to two sentences) and is subordinated to the economic argument rather than dominating it. The device does not distract from the formal modeling or quantitative analysis. The restraint in usage is appropriate: more frequent mentions would risk making the paper feel like a tech demo rather than an academic contribution.

## Overall Assessment
The rhetorical device is well-executed. It appears in the right places, serves the paper's argument, and is used with appropriate restraint. The main risk is the directness of the abstract phrasing ("written entirely by AI agents"), which could trigger reflexive skepticism in referees or editors---especially given the prior arxiv rejection. But the intellectual payoff of the device (the paper is evidence for its own thesis) justifies the risk, and the placement at the end of each section minimizes disruption.
