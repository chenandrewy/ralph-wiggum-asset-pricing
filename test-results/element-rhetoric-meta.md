REASON: The meta-rhetorical device is present in both the abstract and introduction, deployed with enough restraint and economic framing to be compelling without alienating human readers.

## Evaluation

### Element 1: Used in both abstract and introduction? PASS

The device appears in two places:

- **Abstract (final sentence):** "This paper's analysis was itself produced by AI agents, illustrating the displacement it models."
- **Introduction (penultimate paragraph):** "This paper offers a small illustration of the displacement it models. The research, derivations, code, and writing were produced by AI agents working from a human-written economic specification and test scripts."

Both instances are present and clearly deploy the same meta-rhetorical move.

### Element 2: Would humans be turned off? PASS

This is the highest-risk element given the arxiv rejection. The current treatment avoids the most common pitfalls:

- **Positioning:** Both instances appear at the *end* of their respective sections, not as openers. The abstract leads with the economics; the AI disclosure is a coda. The introduction paragraph appears after the full economic argument has been made. A reader who skims will encounter the economic contribution first.
- **Tone:** The language is modest and matter-of-fact. "Small illustration" in the introduction is deliberately understated. There is no self-congratulation, no claims about AI quality, no promotional language.
- **Framing:** The disclosure is framed as *evidence for the economic argument*, not as a novelty or stunt. "Illustrating the displacement it models" ties the meta-commentary directly to the paper's thesis. This gives the reader an economic reason to care about the disclosure rather than treating it as a gimmick.
- **Brevity:** One sentence in the abstract, three sentences in the introduction. The paper does not dwell.

The risk is not zero---some readers will always be skeptical of AI-written work---but the treatment is restrained enough that it reads as a substantive point rather than a red flag.

### Element 3: Compelling and interesting? PASS

The device is genuinely clever. A paper that models AI displacement risk and is itself an instance of AI displacement creates a recursive illustration that strengthens the argument. The introduction paragraph makes the connection explicit: "If the boundary of what AI can produce continues to expand, the displacement risks studied here become increasingly concrete." This turns the meta-commentary into forward-looking economic reasoning rather than mere self-reference.

The description of the division of labor (human wrote the economic specification and test scripts; AI did everything else) is specific and credible, which makes the illustration more concrete than a vague "AI helped write this."

### Element 4: Distracting or overbearing? PASS

The device is confined to:
- 1 sentence in the abstract (out of ~5 sentences)
- 1 short paragraph in the introduction (out of ~7 paragraphs)

It does not appear in the model section, extensions, or conclusion. It does not interrupt the flow of the economic argument. The abstract version is a single clause appended to the end. The introduction version is a self-contained paragraph that a reader could skip without losing any economic content. The paper does not repeatedly remind the reader that it was AI-produced.
