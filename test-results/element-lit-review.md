## 3. Missing References by Topic Area

### Displacement risk / intergenerational asset pricing

- **Garleanu & Panageas (2015, JPE)** — "Young, Old, Conservative, and Bold." Extends the OLG asset pricing framework with preference heterogeneity. By two of the three GKP authors, same OLG setting. **Classification: MINOR.** The mechanism (preference heterogeneity) differs from displacement risk; GKP 2012 covers the relevant insight.

- **Garleanu & Panageas (2023, JPE)** — "Heterogeneity and Asset Prices: An Intergenerational Approach." Shows intergenerational consumption differences drive asset pricing even without aggregate risk. **Classification: MINOR.** Related OLG framework but different channel (cohort consumption growth vs. displacement). The paper's narrow focus on displacement risk is well-served by citing GKP 2012 directly.

### Innovation and asset prices

- **Kung & Schmid (2015, JF)** — "Innovation, Growth, and Asset Prices." Endogenous growth through R&D generates long-run risk in equilibrium. **Classification: MINOR.** Different mechanism (long-run risk from innovation) than displacement risk. Kogan & Papanikolaou (2014) and Kogan, Papanikolaou, Stoffman (2020) already cover the innovation/asset pricing intersection more directly.

### Creative destruction and entry

- **Loualiche (2021, JF)** — "Asset Pricing with Entry and Imperfect Competition." New firm entry as a priced risk factor. **Classification: MINOR.** Tangentially related through creative destruction, but the displacement mechanism differs. Already covered by KPS (2020).

### AI and singularity economics

- **Nordhaus (2021, AEJ: Macroeconomics)** — "Are We Approaching an Economic Singularity?" Tests whether IT/AI will trigger a growth singularity. **Classification: MINOR.** Relevant framing but (a) not in the target journal set, and (b) the paper's AI economics discussion already cites Jones (2024) and Korinek & Suh (2024), which are more directly relevant to the model's extension.
